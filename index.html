Some common questions
were often asked

What is diabetic retinopathy, and why is early detection important?

Diabetic retinopathy is a diabetes-related eye disease that damages blood vessels in the retina, potentially leading to blindness. Early detection is crucial because timely intervention can prevent vision loss and improve outcomes

How does the quantum-accelerated classification algorithm work?

The quantum-accelerated classification algorithm utilizes the power of quantum computing to enhance the accuracy of diabetic retinopathy detection. It processes fundus images and employs quantum techniques to optimize complex computations, resulting in more accurate disease grade classification.

Can I upload fundus images taken with any device?

Yes, the application supports uploading fundus images captured using different devices. However, for optimal accuracy, it's recommended to upload high-quality images taken with specialized retinal imaging equipment.

Can patients access their own medical records through the application?

Yes, patients can securely access their medical records, view their fundus images, and understand their disease grade classifications. The application empowers patients with valuable information about their diabetic retinopathy status.

How can medical professionals benefit from this application?

Medical professionals can efficiently manage patient medical records, perform accurate diabetic retinopathy assessments, and receive timely insights for diagnosis and treatment planning. The application streamlines the process and enhances decision-making.

Are the classification results from the algorithm reliable?

Yes, the classification results are reliable and validated based on a robust set of performance metrics, including accuracy, precision, recall, specificity, and F1-score. The quantum-accelerated algorithm consistently achieves high accuracy levels.
